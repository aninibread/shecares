Fast Facts




With the rise of increasingly polarizing views on women's sexual health, it is important that every woman has convenient access to information regarding their health and well being. SheCares hopes to educate the general public on health topics ranging from pain management to contraceptives. The following is a list of interesting facts on female reproductive health:

  • In 2016, more than 30% of women were using both condoms and oral contraceptives, with the highest rate of use being among women aged 15 to 19 (47%).
  • Women over 21 who are sexually active should visit a gynecologist to have a Pap smear test once every three years.
  • Sexually transmitted infections (STIs) are more common than one may think. One in two sexually active persons will contract an STI by age 25.
  • Planned Parenthood is a great resource. Statistics show they prevent roughly 550,000 unintended pregnancies per year.
